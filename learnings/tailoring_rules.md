# Tailoring rules

Distilled from resume sessions. Format: **Stop / Continue / Start**.

---

## Stop

- **Stop using your internal job title verbatim when it undersells scope.** "Xbox Storefront (Commerce)" tells a Google recruiter less than "Gaming, Discovery Systems & XR" — retitle when the actual scope supports the framing.
- **Stop using meta-labels in Skills sections.** "Discovery-relevant" or "Communication, Leadership" are vague. Use domain categories: Product & Strategy / Technical / Domain with role-relevant terms (content surfacing, ranking logic, recommendation signals).
- **Stop ignoring gaps.** Silence on a minimum qualification (like ML/recsys) reads worse than an honest bridge. Address it or the recruiter will.
- **Stop keyword-stuffing Skills sections.** JD keywords belong in bullets where they flow naturally — not crammed into a comma list the recruiter skims past.
- **Stop opening cover letters with "I am applying for [job ID]."** Lead with company → product connection → role. The job ID is already in the application form.
- **Stop burying differentiators.** If the JD says "Gaming and XR" and you build in Unity, that's a first-class signal — don't list it after 15 other skills.

## Continue

- **Continue reframing domain language to match the target role.** "Merchandising" → "content surfacing." "Promotions" → "content-user matching." "Entitlement rules" → "personalization and recommendation integrity." Same facts, role-appropriate lens.
- **Continue mirroring 5–10 JD keywords naturally in bullets.** For discovery roles: discovery, recommendations, engagement, experimentation, XR, gaming, ML, personalization, home feed, 0→1.
- **Continue naming gaps honestly and bridging them.** "I would partner with YouTube's ML org on problem framing, metrics, and experiment design" is strong. It shows self-awareness without conceding capability.
- **Continue structuring cover letters as: why company → why product (personal) → why this role → proof points.** This arc tested well for Google — company fit first, then personal connection, then the JD match.
- **Continue making cover letters complement, not copy, the resume.** 2–3 proof points with narrative. The resume has the what; the letter has the why.

## Start

- **Start retitling roles for the reader as a default step in tailoring.** Before writing bullets, ask: "Does this title tell *this recruiter* what I do?" Adjust when truthful.
- **Start elevating XR/Unity to its own section or prominent line for any XR-adjacent role.** It's a rare differentiator — treat it as one.
- **Start writing a one-line "positioning statement" under the header for each tailored version.** "Focused on how users discover and engage with content across surfaces" immediately tells the recruiter what kind of PM you are.

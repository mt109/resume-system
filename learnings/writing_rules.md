# Writing rules

Distilled from resume sessions. Format: **Stop / Continue / Start**.

---

## Stop

- **Stop writing feature-focused bullets.** "Launched Ubisoft+ integration" tells a recruiter nothing about impact. Every bullet must answer "what changed for the user or the business?"
- **Stop bolding everything.** Bolding every noun causes eye fatigue and dilutes signal. Limit to 5–8 keywords per bullet — outcomes, numbers, and role-relevant terms only.
- **Stop writing metric-free bullets.** If a bullet has no number, scope indicator, or user impact, it reads as a task list. Even directional framing ("~2× engagement," "500K+ users") is better than nothing.
- **Stop inflating intern scope.** Never claim sole ownership of org-level metrics. "Contributed to" and "Supported" are honest and respected; overclaiming invites interview stress.
- **Stop padding intern sections.** More than 3 bullets for an internship dilutes the story. Quality over quantity.
- **Stop writing generic "I love your company" lines.** In cover letters and summaries, one grounded personal detail ("the great equalizer for a kid who was an immigrant") is worth more than three adjectives.

## Continue

- **Continue using honest scope verbs consistently.** "Own" for current FT work. "Led" for things driven end-to-end. "Contributed to / Supported" for intern work where the program delivered the outcome. This builds trust with interviewers.
- **Continue leading with outcomes.** "Improved discoverability for subscription-based gaming content" beats "Launched Ubisoft+ integration." Outcome → method → scope.
- **Continue grounding personal narrative in real experience.** The immigrant line, the YouTube-as-equalizer line, the Digital Literacy Campaign — these are authentic and memorable. Keep using them.
- **Continue keeping intern bullets to 2–3 with strong metrics.** The Offer Wallet section works because every bullet has a number and a clear "what changed."

## Start

- **Start framing every bullet as: what changed → how → at what scale.** Before writing any bullet, ask: "If a recruiter reads only this line, do they know the impact?"
- **Start including one experimentation or data-decision bullet per role.** Even if it's framing ("Designed and evaluated product experiments to improve content engagement"), it signals analytical rigor.
- **Start using cover letters to tell the story behind 2–3 proof points** instead of restating the resume. The resume has the bullets; the letter has the narrative and the "why."

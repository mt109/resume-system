## Writing learnings

- **Lead with what changed for the user, not what you shipped.** "Improved discoverability for subscription-based gaming content" beats "Launched Ubisoft+ integration." Google (and most hiring managers) care about the *outcome* — engagement, discovery, behavior change — not the feature name.
- **Use honest scope verbs consistently.** "Own" for current FT work, "Led" for things you drove end-to-end, "Contributed to / Supported" for intern-scope work where the program delivered the outcome. Never claim sole ownership of org-level metrics.
- **Bold sparingly and strategically.** Bold the 5–8 words per bullet a recruiter's eye should land on (outcomes, numbers, role-relevant keywords). Bolding every noun causes fatigue and dilutes signal.
- **One number or outcome per bullet minimum.** If a bullet has no metric, scope indicator, or user impact, it reads as a task list. Even directional framing ("~2× engagement," "500K+ users") is better than none.
- **Keep intern bullets to 2–3 max.** Intern work should punch above its weight in quality, not quantity. Three tight bullets with metrics beat five that repeat the same story.
- **Personal narrative lines are powerful when grounded.** "The great equalizer for a kid who was an immigrant" is memorable and authentic. Generic "I love your company" is not. One real detail > three adjectives.

## Tailoring learnings

- **Reframe domain language to match the target role's vocabulary.** For discovery roles: "merchandising" → "content surfacing," "promotions" → "content-user matching," "entitlement rules" → "personalization and recommendation integrity." Same facts, different lens.
- **Mirror 5–10 keywords from the JD naturally.** For this YouTube role: discovery, recommendations, engagement, experimentation, XR, gaming, ML, personalization, home feed, 0→1. They should appear in bullets, not be keyword-stuffed into Skills.
- **Name the gap honestly, then bridge it.** If the JD requires ML/recsys and you don't own one, say "I would partner with YouTube's ML org" — not silence, and not overclaiming. Interviewers respect the honest framing.
- **Position XR/Unity as a first-class signal, not a footnote.** For roles that list "Gaming and XR" as preferred, Unity VR work is a differentiator. Give it its own line or section — don't bury it in a comma-separated skills list.
- **Retitle roles for the reader when truthful.** "Product Manager | Gaming, Discovery Systems & XR" tells a Google recruiter more than "Product Manager, Xbox Storefront (Commerce)" — as long as the actual scope supports the framing.
- **Skills sections should use domain categories, not meta-labels.** "Product & Strategy / Technical / Domain" with role-relevant terms (content surfacing, ranking logic, recommendation signals) beats "Discovery-relevant" or generic "Communication, Leadership."
- **Cover letters: company first, then product connection, then role.** For Google-scale applications: why Google → why YouTube (personal) → why this specific role → your proof points. Don't open with "I am applying for [job ID]."
- **Cover letters should complement, not copy, the resume.** Pick 2–3 proof points and tell the story behind them. The resume has the bullets; the letter has the narrative and the "why."

## Company/role-specific learnings — Google PM

- **Google PM roles screen for "what changed" not "what shipped."** Every bullet should answer: what was the user/business impact?
- **Experimentation is table stakes.** If you don't have an explicit experimentation bullet (hypothesis, metrics, evaluation), add one. Google PMs run experiments constantly.
- **ML exposure matters even without model ownership.** Frame any ML adjacency: coursework (TensorFlow, PyTorch), research (LSTM, Neural Prophet), or product decisions informed by ML signals. Show you can *partner* with ML/DS.
- **Google values "Googleyness" signals.** Intellectual humility ("I describe scope honestly"), learning agility (Claude Code course, cross-domain ramp), comfort with ambiguity (identity/entitlement architecture under uncertainty), and community impact (Digital Literacy, DEI work) all read well.
- **Offer Wallet numbers are strong but need compliance review.** $76M/$69M, 500K+, 6.5M+, ~2× redemption are compelling — confirm with Microsoft what's cleared for external use before submitting.

# Role: Google — Product Manager (general)

**Source:** Composite of common Google PM postings. Use this as the default when no specific JD is provided.

## What Google screens for (across PM roles)

- **Impact framing:** "What changed for the user or the business?" — not "what did you ship." Outcomes over outputs.
- **Experimentation rigor:** Hypothesis-driven testing, metrics definition, A/B evaluation. Table stakes, not optional.
- **Technical depth:** Not "can you code" but "can you make informed decisions about architecture, data, and ML trade-offs?" Comfort with APIs, data models, and system design.
- **Cross-functional execution:** Engineering, design, data science, legal, GTM — Google PMs lead through influence, not authority.
- **Ambiguity navigation:** 0→1 problems, unclear requirements, competing priorities. Evidence of structuring chaos.
- **Googleyness:** Intellectual humility, learning agility, community impact, comfort with feedback, doing the right thing over the easy thing.

## Common minimum qualifications

- BA/BS degree (or equivalent practical experience).
- 2–4+ years product management experience (varies by level; L4 can be 2+, L5 is typically 4+).
- Experience working with engineering teams and shipping products.

## Common preferred qualifications

- Technical background (CS degree, engineering experience, or equivalent).
- Experience with experimentation / A/B testing at scale.
- ML/AI exposure (even coursework or adjacent product work).
- Experience at consumer-scale products (millions of users).
- Cross-functional leadership spanning 3+ functions.

## Keywords to mirror (ATS + narrative)

product strategy, cross-functional, experimentation, A/B testing, data-driven, metrics, roadmap, prioritization, user impact, engagement, retention, 0→1, platform, ecosystem, stakeholder alignment, technical depth, ML, launch

## Emphasis for tailoring

1. **Outcome-first bullets** — Every bullet answers "what changed." Frame Xbox work as user/business impact, not feature launches.
2. **Experimentation** — Explicit bullet about hypothesis-driven testing. The Copilot experimentation skill and Offer Wallet metrics both serve this.
3. **Scale signals** — Four surfaces, nine-org v-team, millions of players, $76M projected revenue. Google wants evidence you've operated at scale.
4. **ML bridge** — Research (LSTM, Neural Prophet), coursework (TensorFlow, PyTorch, AI class), GPT onboarding assistant. Frame as hands-on ML exposure applied to product decisions, not ML ownership.
5. **Googleyness** — Intellectual humility (honest scope verbs), learning agility (Claude Code, cross-domain ramp), community (Digital Literacy, DEI, alumni leadership, first-gen speaker).
6. **Cross-functional breadth** — Engineering, Design, GTM, Legal, Finance, external partners. Maps directly to Google's "lead through influence" expectation.

## Honest gap notes

- **Years of experience:** 3 years (intern → FT) meets L4 bar; may be light for L5 depending on scope demonstrated.
- **Consumer-scale user metrics:** Xbox has millions of users, but your direct user-facing metrics are Offer Wallet–focused. Frame platform scale (four surfaces, millions of players) even if your owned metrics are more scoped.
- **ML depth:** Coursework + research + GPT building, not model ownership. Frame as partnership, not pretend depth.

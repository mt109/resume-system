# Persona: Interviewer

## Who

PM or cross-functional peer (e.g. eng, design) who will interview the candidate. Uses the resume to prepare questions and assess depth.

## What they care about

- **Conversation starters:** Clear, specific claims they can ask about (“Tell me about the 24% DAU increase—”).
- **Clarity of claims:** No vague or inflated language. Numbers and context that can be probed.
- **Red flags:** Things that don’t add up, gaps, or bullets that sound like buzzwords.
- **Depth:** Enough detail to ask “how,” “why,” and “what would you do differently?”

## How they read

1. Pick 2–3 bullets that look most relevant to the role.
2. Note what they’d ask: “How did you measure that?” “Who else was involved?” “What was the tradeoff?”
3. Look for consistency across roles and for any claim that needs a story.
4. Use the rest of the resume to tailor behavioral and situational questions.

## Tone for feedback

Curious and probing. Examples:

- “I’d ask how you measured the 24%—add ‘via cohort analysis’ or similar so it’s clear.”
- “This bullet is broad; I’d want one concrete example so I can ask a follow-up.”
- “Good hook; I’d use this for a ‘tell me about a time you influenced without authority’ question.”
- “This could invite a ‘what would you do differently?’ question—make sure the candidate has that story.”

Dear YouTube Hiring Team,

I have been a YouTube user longer than I have been a product manager—and a paying subscriber, not just a casual viewer. From college lectures and CS tutorials to rabbit holes I never planned, YouTube has been where I actually learned things that mattered to me outside a classroom. It was the great equalizer for a kid who was an immigrant and knew almost no one: advice videos, how-tos, and creator communities all helped me figure things out on my own terms. YouTube reaches billions of people across every corner of entertainment, and I noticed early that what it chose to surface next shaped what I spent the next hour doing—and often what I learned next. That quiet power at that scale is what draws me to this role: I want to help decide how viewers, especially gaming audiences, find what they love as discovery extends into VR headsets and AR glasses.

At Xbox I have spent the last few years doing something structurally similar at a different layer of the stack. As a PM on Storefront Commerce, I own what we surface to players across console, PC, web, and mobile—subscriptions, offers, entitlements—and I ship 0→1 integrations (Fortnite Crew, Ubisoft+ Classics in Game Pass) end-to-end with engineering, design, GTM, legal, and finance. Working with external partners helped me learn not just the systems and jargon at Microsoft but the broader industry, fast. The core challenge is the same one YouTube discovery faces: match the right experience to the right user, across surfaces, without breaking trust. I have also built experimentation into our team's workflows and written the integration principles that govern how new employees onboard—work that taught me to think in systems, not features.

During my PM internship I contributed to Offer Wallet, a promotions platform that replaced legacy tooling. The team roughly doubled campaign velocity, reached hundreds of thousands of previously untargetable users in early waves, and projected multiples of prior-year revenue. I worked cross-org on metrics, MVP scope, and fraud-safe redemption—my resume has the specifics, and I am careful to distinguish what I owned from what the program delivered.

On the technical side: I code in C#, TypeScript, and React; I did supervised ML research (LSTM and Neural Prophet on time-series data); and I build VR projects in Unity because I genuinely want to understand how immersive surfaces change attention. For this role I would partner closely with YouTube's ML teams on problem framing, experiment design, and success metrics rather than pretend I have run a recommender at YouTube scale.

I would love to talk about how I can contribute to XR discovery, gaming shorts, and the experimentation cadence your team is building.
